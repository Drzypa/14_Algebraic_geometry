elliptic surface


Definition 1.

Let k be a field and let C/k be a smooth projective curve defined over the field k and has genus g. The function field of C/k will be denoted by K=k⁢(C). An elliptic surface E over the curve C is, by definition, a two-dimensional projective variety together with:

  1. 1.

    A morphism π:ℰ→C such that for all but finitely many points t∈C⁢(k¯), the fiber ℰt=π-1⁢(t) is a non-singular curve of genus 1,

  2. 2.

    A section to π (the zero section) σ0:C→ℰ.

With this definition, E/K may be regarded as an elliptic curve over the field K.

Example 1.

The surface y2=x3+t is an elliptic surface over the curve ℙ1⁢(ℚ). It may be regarded as an elliptic curve over the function field ℚ⁢(t).
