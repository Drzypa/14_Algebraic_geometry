flag variety


Let k be a field, and let V be a vector space over k of dimension n and choose an increasing sequence 𝐢=(i1,…,im), with 1≤i1<⋯<im≤n. Then the (partial) flag variety ℱ⁢ℓ⁢(V,𝐢) associated to this data is the set of all flags {0}≤V1⊂⋯⊂Vn with dim⁡Vj=ij. This has a natural embedding into the product of Grassmannians G⁢(V,i1)×⋯⁢G⁢(V,im), and its image here is closed, making ℱ⁢ℓ⁢(V,𝐢) into a projective variety over k. If k=ℂ these are often called flag manifolds.

The group Sl⁢(V) acts transtively on ℱ⁢ℓ⁢(V,𝐢), and the stabilizer of a point is a parabolic subgroup. Thus, as a homogeneous space, ℱ⁢ℓ⁢(V,𝐢)≅Sl⁢(V)/P where P is a parabolic subgroup of Sl⁢(V). In particular, the complete flag variety is isomorphic to Sl⁢(V)/B, where B is the Borel subgroup.
