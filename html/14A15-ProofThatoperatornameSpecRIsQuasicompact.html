proof that Spec⁡(R) is quasi-compact


Note that most of the notation used here is defined in the entry prime spectrum.

The following is a proof that Spec⁡(R) is quasi-compact.

Proof.

Let Λ be an indexing set and {Uλ}λ∈Λ be an open cover for Spec⁡(R). For every λ∈Λ, let Iλ be an ideal of R with Uλ=Spec⁡(R)∖V⁢(Iλ). Since

Spec⁡(R)=⋃λ∈ΛUλ=⋃λ∈Λ(Spec⁡(R)∖V⁢(Iλ))=Spec⁡(R)∖⋂λ∈ΛV⁢(Iλ)=Spec⁡(R)∖V⁢(∑λ∈ΛIλ),

V⁢(∑λ∈ΛIλ)=∅. Thus, by this theorem (http://planetmath.org/VIemptysetImpliesIR), ∑λ∈ΛIλ=R. Since 1R∈R=∑λ∈ΛIλ, there exists a finite subset L of Λ such that, for every ℓ∈L, there exists an iℓ∈Iℓ with 1R=∑ℓ∈Liℓ.

Let r∈R. Then r=r⋅1R=r⁢∑ℓ∈Liℓ=∑ℓ∈Lr⋅iℓ∈∑ℓ∈LIℓ. Thus, ∑ℓ∈LIℓ=R. Therefore, V⁢(∑ℓ∈LIℓ)=∅. Since

Spec⁡(R)=Spec⁡(R)∖V⁢(∑ℓ∈LIℓ)=Spec⁡(R)∖⋂ℓ∈LV⁢(Iℓ)=⋃ℓ∈L(Spec⁡(R)∖V⁢(Iℓ))=⋃ℓ∈LUℓ,

{Uλ}λ∈Λ restricts to a finite subcover. It follows that Spec⁡(R) is quasi-compact. ∎
