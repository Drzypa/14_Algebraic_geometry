subsheaf of abelian groups


Let ℱ be a sheaf of abelian groups over a topological space X. Let 𝒢 be a sheaf over X, such that for every open set U⊂X, 𝒢⁢(U) is a subgroup of ℱ⁢(U). And further let the on 𝒢 be by those on ℱ. Then 𝒢 is a subsheaf of ℱ.

Suppose a sheaf of abelian groups ℱ is defined as a disjoint union of stalks ℱx over points x∈X, and ℱ is topologized in the appropriate manner. In particular, each stalk is an abelian group and the group operations are continuous. Then a subsheaf 𝒢 is an open subset of ℱ such that 𝒢x=𝒢∩ℱx is a subgroup of ℱx.

When 𝒢 is a subsheaf of ℱ, then ℱx/𝒢x is an abelian group. Considering this to be the stalk over x we have a sheaf which is denoted by ℱ/𝒢, with the topology being the quotient topology.

Example.

Suppose M is a complex manifold. Let M* be the sheaf of germs of meromorphic functions which are not identically zero. That is, for z∈M, the stalk Mz* is the abelian group of germs of meromorphic functions at z with the group operation being multiplication. Then O*, the sheaf of germs of holomorphic functions which are not identically 0 is a subsheaf of M*.

The sheaf M*/O* is then the sheaf of divisors. If M is of (complex) dimension 1, then M*/O* is just the sheaf of functions into the integers with finite support.
