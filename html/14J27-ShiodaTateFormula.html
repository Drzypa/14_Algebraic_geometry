Shioda-Tate formula


The main references for this part are the works of Shioda and Tate [2], [4], [5].

Let k be a field and let k¯ be a fixed algebraic closure of k. Let ℰ be an elliptic surface over a curve C/k and let K=k⁢(C) be the function field of C. Let ℰ¯=ℰ⁢(k¯) (or more precisely ℰ¯=ℰ×Spec⁡kSpec⁡k¯). The Néron-Severi group of ℰ¯, denoted by NS⁡(ℰ¯), is by definition the group of divisors on ℰ¯ modulo algebraic equivalence. Under the previous assumptions, NS⁡(ℰ¯) is a finitely generated abelian group (this is a consequence of the so-called ‘theorem of the base’ which can be found in [1]). The Néron-Severi group of ℰ, denoted by NS⁡(ℰ), is simply the image of the group of divisors on ℰ in NS⁡(ℰ¯). Let T⊂NS⁡(ℰ) be the subgroup generated by the image of the zero-section σ0 and all the irreducible components of the fibers of π. T is sometimes called the “trivial part” of NS⁡(ℰ).

Theorem (Shioda-Tate formula).

For each t∈C let nt be the number of irreducible components on the fiber at t, i.e. π-1⁢(t). Then:

rankℤ⁡(ℰ/K) = rankℤ⁡(NS⁡(ℰ))-rankℤ⁡(T)
= rankℤ⁡(NS⁡(ℰ))-2-∑t∈C(nt-1).
