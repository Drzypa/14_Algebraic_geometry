ind-variety


Let 𝕂 be a field. An ind-variety over 𝕂 is a set X along with a filtration:

X0⊂X1⊂⋯⁢Xn⊂⋯

such that

  1. 1.

    X=⋃j≥0Xj

  2. 2.

    Each Xi is a finite dimensional algebraic variety over 𝕂

  3. 3.

    The inclusions ij:Xj→Xj+1 are closed embeddings of algebraic varieties

The ring of regular functions on an ind-variety X is defined to be 𝕂⁡[X]:=lim←⁡𝕂⁡[Xj] where the limit is taken with respect to the family of maps {ij*:𝕂⁡[Xj+1]→𝕂⁡[Xj]}j≥0.

This ring is given the structure of a topological ring by letting each 𝕂⁡[Xj] have the discrete topology and 𝕂⁡[X] have the induced inverse limit topology, i.e. the topology induced from the canonical inclusion lim←⁡𝕂⁡[Xj]⊂∏j𝕂⁡[Xj] and the product topology on ∏j𝕂⁡[Xj].

An ind-variety is called affine (resp. projective) if each Xj is affine (resp. projective).

The notion of an ind-variety goes back to Igor Shafarevich in [3] and [4].

Examples

Let 𝒦:=𝕂⁡((t)) be the ring of formal Laurant series over 𝕂 and 𝒪:=𝕂⁡[[t]] be its ring of integers, the formal Taylor series. Let V=𝕂n. Then the set X of 𝒪-lattices (𝒪-submodules of maximal rank) in V⊗𝕂𝒦 is an example of a (non-finite dimensional) projective ind-variety using the filtration

Xi:={L∈X∣ti⁢L0⊂L⊂t-i⁢L0,dim𝕂⁡L/ti⁢L0=i⁢n}

where L0:=V⊗𝕂𝒪.

(cf. [1] section 11, or [2] appendix C part 7)
