projective variety


Given a homogeneous polynomial F of degree d in n+1 variables X0,…,Xn and a point [x0:⋯:xn], we cannot evaluate F at that point, because it has multiple such representations, but since F⁢(λ⁢x0,…,λ⁢xn)=λd⁢F⁢(x0,…,xn) we can say whether any such representation (and hence all) vanish at that point.

A projective variety over an algebraically closed field k is a subset of some projective space ℙkn over k which can be described as the common vanishing locus of finitely many homogeneous polynomials with coefficients in k, and which is not the union of two such smaller loci. Also, a quasi-projective variety is an open subset of a projective variety.
