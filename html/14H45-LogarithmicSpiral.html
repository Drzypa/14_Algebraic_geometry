logarithmic spiral


The equation of the logarithmic spiral in polar coordinates r,φ is

r=C⁢ek⁢φ (1)

where C and k are constants (C>0).  Thus the position vector of the point of this curve as the coordinate vector is written as

r→=(C⁢ek⁢φ⁢cos⁡φ,C⁢ek⁢φ⁢sin⁡φ)

which is a parametric form of the curve.

Perhaps the most known of the logarithmic spiral is that any line emanating from the origin the curve under a constant angle ψ.  This is seen e.g. by using the vector r→ and its derivative  d⁢r→d⁢φ=r→′,  the latter of which gives the direction of the tangent line (see vector-valued function):

r→′=(C⁢ek⁢φ⁢k⁢cos⁡φ-C⁢ek⁢φ⁢sin⁡φ,C⁢ek⁢φ⁢k⁢sin⁡φ+C⁢ek⁢φ⁢cos⁡φ).

One obtains

r→⋅r→′=k⁢r2,|r→|=r,|r→′|=r⁢1+k2,

whence

cos⁡ψ=r→⋅r→′|r→|⁢|r→′|=k1+k2=constant.

It follows that  k=cot⁡ψ.  The angle ψ is called the polar tangential angle.

The logarithmic spiral (1) goes infinitely many times round the origin without to reach it; in the case  k>0  one may state that

limφ→-∞⁡C⁢ek⁢φ= 0 but C⁢ek⁢φ≠ 0⁢∀φ∈ℝ

(the exponential function never vanishes).

The arc length s of the logarithmic spiral is expressible in closed form; if we take it for the interval  [φ1,φ2],  we can calculate in the case  k>0  that

s=∫φ1φ2r2+(d⁢rd⁢φ)2⁢𝑑φ=∫φ1φ2C2⁢e2⁢k⁢φ+C2⁢e2⁢k⁢φ⁢k2⁢𝑑φ=1+k2k⁢C⁢(ek⁢φ2-ek⁢φ1),

thus

s=1+k2k⁢(r2-r1)=r2-r1cos⁡ψ.

Letting  φ1→-∞  one sees that the arc length from the origin to a point of the spiral is finite.

Other properties

  • •

    Any curve with constant polar tangential angle is a logarithmic spiral.

  • •

    All logarithmic spirals with equal polar tangential angle are similar.

  • •

    A logarithmic spiral rotated about the origin is a spiral homothetic to the original one.

  • •

    The inversion  z↦1z  causes for the logarithmic spiral a reflexion against the imaginary axis and a rotation around the origin, but the image is congruent to the original one.

  • •

    The evolute of the logarithmic spiral is a congruent logarithmic spiral.

  • •

    The catacaustic of the logarithmic spiral is a logarithmic spiral.

  • •

    The families  r=C1⁢eφ  and  r=C2⁢e-φ  are orthogonal curves to each other.
