algebraic manifold


Definition.

Let k be a field and let M⊂kn be a submanifold. M is said to be an algebraic manifold (or k-algebraic) if there exists an irreducible algebraic variety V⊂kn such that dim⁡V=dim⁡M and M⊂V. If k=ℝ, then M is called a Nash manifold.

It can be proved that such a manifold is defined as the zero set of a finite collection of analytic algebraic functions.
