Nagell-Lutz theorem


The following theorem, proved independently by E. Lutz and T. Nagell, gives a very efficient method to compute the torsion subgroup of an elliptic curve defined over ℚ.

Theorem 1 (Nagell-Lutz Theorem).

Let E/Q be an elliptic curve with Weierstrass equation:

y2=x3+A⁢x+B,A,B∈ℤ

Then for all non-zero torsion points P we have:

  1. 1.

    The coordinates of P are in ℤ, i.e.

    x⁢(P),y⁢(P)∈ℤ
  2. 2.

    If P is of order greater than 2, then

  3. 3.

    If P is of order 2 then

    y⁢(P)=0 a⁢n⁢d x⁢(P)3+A⁢x⁢(P)+B=0
